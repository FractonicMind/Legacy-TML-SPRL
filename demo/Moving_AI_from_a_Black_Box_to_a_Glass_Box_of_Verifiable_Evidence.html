Ternary Moral Logic (TML)

Moving AI from a Black Box to a Glass Box of Verifiable Evidence

The Core Architecture: A Simple, Powerful Decision

TML replaces ambiguity with a clear, three-state system for every AI action, determined by its potential risk. This ensures a proportional response, preventing both operational silence and excessive logging.

-1

Prohibit

Blocks unsafe or malicious actions. The refusal is logged as a permanent record of prevented harm.

0

Pause

The "Sacred Pause" is triggered for ethically complex actions, creating a detailed audit log of the AI's reasoning, alternatives, and risks.

+1

Proceed

Allows routine, low-risk actions to execute with minimal metadata, ensuring speed and efficiency.

The Decision Engine: Stakeholder Proportional Risk Level (SPRL)

The decision to Proceed, Pause, or Prohibit isn't arbitrary. It's calculated using the SPRL, a metric derived from an action's potential impact, likelihood of harm, and severity of harm to all stakeholders.

Visualizing a High-Risk Scenario

In this example, a high SPRL score (8.2/10) would automatically trigger a Prohibit state, preventing a potentially dangerous action from occurring.

A Framework of Accountability

TML defines clear lines of responsibility, ensuring that failures are not abstract errors but specific, actionable liabilities with consequences for developers, operators, and executives.

Breakdown of AI Liability

TML assigns liability based on the type of failure, creating a powerful incentive for responsible AI development and deployment. This transparent model ensures that victims have clear recourse.

  • Mis-set thresholds = Negligence
  • Missing logs = Automatic Liability
  • Log manipulation = Fraud
  • Gaming proportionality = Executive Accountability
